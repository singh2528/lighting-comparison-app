Types of fixtures

The six fixture types behind almost every lighting plan

Most homes use a mix of these — general lighting for everyday use, and decorative or accent layers on top for mood and design.

⚪

Downlights & spotlights

7-15W · 90-1350 lumens · 24°-110° beam. Fixed for even general lighting, gimbal/adjustable for highlighting art and walls. The workhorse of most homes.

▭

Panel & surface lights

18-40W · 1800-4000 lumens · 120°+ spread. Flat LED panels for even, shadow-free light — kitchens, utility areas, bathrooms and offices.

┄

Track lighting

7-12W per head · 15°-36° beam · 1-3m rails. Repositionable spotlight heads on a power rail — feature walls, galleries, kitchen islands.

〜

Cove & strip LED

4.8-14.4W per metre · 300-1100 lumens/m · 2700-3000K, dimmable. Hidden in false-ceiling coves, pelmets and under cabinets for glare-free ambient light.

✺

Pendants & chandeliers

Hang 70-90cm above a table or island. Decorative focal point — chosen for look first, but still counted toward the room's lux target.

☀

Outdoor & façade lighting

IP65+ rated · 3-12W per fixture. Wall washers, in-ground uplights, step lights and bollards for gardens, terraces and façades — anything exposed to weather.

Technical specs that matter

The numbers that separate good fixtures from bad ones

Wattage on a box tells you almost nothing. These are the specs that actually determine how a light looks, performs and lasts.

💡

Lumens (brightness)

Actual light output, not watts. Two fixtures rated the same wattage can differ by 500+ lumens — this is the number lux calculations are based on.

🎨

CRI (colour accuracy)

0-100 scale. Below 80, skin tones and food look slightly "off". 90+ reads true to daylight — worth it for kitchens, wardrobes and feature areas.

🌡️

CCT (warm vs cool)

2700-3000K = warm, relaxing (living rooms, bedrooms). 4000K = crisp, alert (kitchens, study). Tunable-white fixtures shift between both.

📐

Beam angle

15-24° = focused accent. 90-120° = even general spread. Get it wrong and you get hotspots or dead corners.

⚡

Efficacy (lm/W)

Lumens per watt. Today's LEDs range 80-130 lm/W — higher means more brightness for the same running cost.

🎛️

Driver & dimming

The driver is the fixture's power supply — its quality sets lifespan and surge protection. TRIAC, 0-10V or DALI dimming must match across fixture, driver and switch, or you get flicker, buzz or no dimming.
