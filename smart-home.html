Smart Home

Every system, designed to work as one

A smart home is more than smart lighting. It's lighting, entertainment, climate, security and curtains, all responding to the same scenes and schedules — instead of five separate apps that don't talk to each other.

01 · Lighting control

The layer everything else builds on

What it includes

Smart switches and dimmers — or, for full systems, DALI/KNX keypads and a central processor — that replace simple on/off with scenes ("Morning", "Movie night", "Away"), schedules, and app or voice control across every room.

Why it matters

This is usually the first system designed, because the choice here — retrofit smart switches on existing wiring vs. a first-fix bus system — sets the cost and flexibility ceiling for everything that connects to it later: curtains, AC and AV scenes all hang off the lighting layer.

02 · AV & entertainment

Multi-room audio, home theatre and TV — in your scenes, not a drawer of remotes

What it includes

Ceiling or in-wall speakers wired to a multi-zone amplifier for whole-home audio; a soundbar or AV receiver for the main TV/home theatre; and a single app or universal remote that controls TV, streaming boxes and volume across rooms.

Why it matters

Speaker wiring, AV rack location and cable routing need to be planned before walls and false ceilings close — retrofitting later means visible cabling or wireless compromises. Tied into lighting, a "Movie night" scene can dim lights, close curtains and switch the TV input in one tap.

03 · Climate / HVAC

Comfort on a schedule — and your biggest energy line item, automated

What it includes

Smart thermostats or zone sensors layered onto your existing AC/VRF system, scheduling (pre-cool before you arrive, set back overnight), and occupancy-based control so empty rooms aren't cooled unnecessarily.

Why it matters

HVAC is typically the largest ongoing utility cost in a home — even basic scheduling and zoning can meaningfully cut bills. Zoning needs to be planned alongside your ductwork or VRF layout, and pairs naturally with curtains (close before peak afternoon heat) and lighting scenes.

04 · CCTV & security

Cameras, access and alarms — one app, not three

What it includes

Indoor and outdoor cameras with night vision and motion alerts, smart door locks or access control, door/window sensors with an alarm panel, and video intercom for the gate or main door — all viewable from one app.

Why it matters

Camera cabling (PoE) and intercom wiring need to be planned at first fix — running cable later means surface conduit. Decide upfront whether footage is stored locally or in the cloud, since that affects both cost and privacy. Integrated with lighting, a motion alert at night can trigger lights automatically.

05 · Curtains & blinds

Motorised, scheduled, and part of every scene

What it includes

Motorised curtain tracks or blind motors (wired or rechargeable), controlled by switch, app, schedule or scene — open automatically at sunrise, close at sunset, or react to a sun/light sensor.

Why it matters

Track length and curtain weight determine the right motor — get this wrong and the motor strains or the curtain doesn't fully open/close. Wired motors need cabling planned before the curtain box or false ceiling is finished; retrofitting wired tracks afterwards means exposed wiring.

How it all connects

Two ways to integrate these systems

Every home falls into one of two integration approaches — the right one depends on whether you're retrofitting a finished home or designing in from first fix.

Retrofit: Wi-Fi / Zigbee smart devices

Smart switches, plugs, sensors and motors added on top of existing wiring. Each device connects to a hub or directly to Wi-Fi, controlled through an app with voice assistant support. No rewiring needed — ideal for apartments, rentals and renovations where walls are already closed.

First-fix: DALI / KNX-based bus systems

Lighting, curtains, climate, AV and security wired to a dedicated control bus and programmed centrally from one or more processors. Scenes and schedules span every system from a single design. Requires planning before first fix, but gives the tightest integration and the most reliable long-term performance — typical for villas and premium apartments.
